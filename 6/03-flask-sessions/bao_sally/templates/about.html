Sometimes animals are animals.
Other times, they are actually ninjas in disguise.

It looks like a duck. It quacks like a duck.
But it's not a duck!

These animals are employing mimicry (and camouflage).
There are various reasons animals do this.

Take the false cleanerfish for example.



Like its name implies, it is a terrible, terrible person that exploits its kind for personal gain.





Here, the cleaner wrasse and its benefactor are engaged in a beautiful act of symbiosis.
The wrasse, being a bro, is cleaning the other fish's teeth in return for a meal.


Then comes the false cleanerfish, sidling up to one of the wrasse's friends.
It squirrels its way into the fish's mouth and rips out a chunk of meat.

Truly a vile and terrible creature.
